## Lanes

    # Types:
        - marked: lanes are separated by lines. most common
        - unmarked: if there is enough room to drive four wheel vehicle, that is also considered lane.
    
    # Car position:
        * Basic rule is to be in the middle of the lane. 
        * When approaching a wide incoming vehicle, you can move to the right
        * If good visibility ahead but poor visibility on the side, move to left to your lane. Avoid if oncoming traffic.
        
        # When turning:
            - Left turn: close to the left edge. don't obstacle oncoming traffic
            - Right turn: close to the right edge of the carriageway

        # When turning on one way road:
            - Left turn: close to left of the carriageway
            - Right turn: close to right of the carriageway
    
    # Lane to choose:
        * The basic rule is to choose the furthest to the right
        # If following condition met, choose the suitable:
            - Speed limit is less than 70km/h, there are at least two marked lanes in your direction
            - The lanes lead to different destinations
    
    # Prohibition on changing lanes:
        - not allowed to slalom between cars
        - cannot cross solid line

    # Public transport lane (bus lane):
        - Bicycles and class 2 mopeds may also use a public trasport lane
        - If other transport is permitted it's specified in additional panel
        - You may cross a public transport lane to change lane
    
    # Reversible lane
        - direction can be changed


## Priority rules
    * No rights, only obligation

    # Obligation to give away
        - let the intersecting vehicle pass
        - clearly show intention; break in good time
        - stop only if necessary
    
        ** Your obligation to give way applies to all traffc on the entire road you are joining.

    # Obligation to stop
        - Same as the obligation to give away but you have to stop
        - Need to stop completely, cannot creep foward.
        - Stop just before the stop line. If there is no stop line, stop right before the intersecting road.
        The sign can be several meters before the junction.
        - if there is a queue, each car must stop.
        - all way stop mean that all the adjoining roads have an obligation to stop. Who stops first goes first.

    # Priority Roads:
        - all car entering priority road must give way to vehicles already on it.
        - Indicated by sign. The sign displayed after every junction unless it's evident that the priority road
        continues after the junction
    
    # Priority to the right rule:
        - give away to vihecles from the right
        - junction sign
        - everytime vehicle pass, it's default rule unless otherwise stated
    
    # Priority to the right rule does not apply:
        - On priority roads
        - Functioning traffic signals
        - at roundabouts
        - where give away or stop sign is displayed
        - when driver leaves an acceleration lane
        - when reversing
        - at exits
    
    # The turning rule
        - Don't obstruct oncoming traffic
        - Don't obstruct road users on the carriageway you are joining. 
            * This includes pedestrians, cyclists even though there is no crossing.
            * Don't have to stop and wait for pedestrians who are approaching the junction and are about to cross as 
            you must do at pedestrian crossing. 
    
    # The exit rule:
        * You have an obligation to give away when exiting from:
        - A car park
        - a property, a petrol station, or any other similar area in connection to the road
        - A pedestrian street, home zone, bycycle street, hard shoulder, or off road area
        - an uninterrupted footpath or bicycle path that you have crossed. At a junction usually a footpath or bycycle 
        path ends and starts again

    # The obstruction rule:
        - Try to never stop at junction, on a pedestrian crossing or similar
    
    # The bus rule:
        - 50km/h or slower: you must give away to the bys if it indicates to exit. Only applicable to the lane 
        farthest to the right.
        - over 50km: bus must give away to you 
    
    # Give away and do not obstruct:
        - Emergency vehicles (ambulances, police cars, and fire truck) with sirens and/or flashing blue lights turned on.
        - Trains and trams
        - military convoy
        - processions such as children with teacheers and funeral processions
    
    # Signals:
        - red: stop at stop line
        - red + yellow: the change to a green signal is imminent. otherwise it's a red
        - amber: stop if no danger
        - flasing amber: signals not working. take special caution
        - green: 
            - intersecting traffic has red light. 
            - oncoming traffic has green light. don't obstruct when turning.
            - if you turn, pedestrian and cyclists also have green light the road you want to join. You must let them 
            pass
        - green arrow: other traffic (car/pedestrian/cyclists) have red
        - regular signal + arrow: arrow overrides regular signal


## Crossings
    # Pedestrian crossings
        * Pedestrian crossing is indicated by sign and/or road marking

        # In addition to those walking, pedestrians also include:
            - Person in wheelchair (including electric)
            - Persons on roller skates, roller skis, kicksled, toy vehicles and similar
            - Persons walk with a bycycle or moped
        
        * Seek eye contact
        * don't wave the pedestrian
    
        # Controlled pedestrian crossing
            - have functional signals
            - if pedestrian is entered crossing, let them pass
        
        # Uncontrolled pedestrian crossing
            - give away to are on the crossing or about to do so.
            - show clear intention by stoping in good time.
    
    # Footpath:
        - drivers are only allowed to cross he footpath
        - obligation to give way to pedestrians on the footpath
        - lacks standardises design, sometimes (not always) marked with footpath sign
        - often crosses when entering/exiting a property
    
    # Bicycle passages:
        - always indicated by road marking
        - often combined with pedestrian crossings
    
        # Controlled:
            - have functioning traffic signals
            - if they entered when they had green light, give away
        
        # Uncontrolled:
            - adapt your speed, don't endgander those on the bicycle passage
            - when crossing a uncrontrolled biccle passage while turning or exiting a roundabout, you must drive at a 
            low speed and provide any cyclists who are already on the or about to enter with opportunity to pass
            - cyclists have an obligation to give away to drivers

    # Bicycle crossing:
        - always indicated by road sign for bicycle crossing, the road marking for bycycle crossing and give away sign
        - you have obligation to give away to cyclists
    
    * Bicycle crossing and path also applies to electric scooter or class 2 mopeds

    # Bicycle paths:
        - A road or part of a road for bicycles. 
        - Only allowed to cross the bicycle path
        - car have obligation to give way to the cyclists
