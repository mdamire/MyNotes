## Alcohol

    # Drunk driving:
        - 0.2 per mille of alcohol in blood; 0.1 mg of alcohol per litter of breath
        - fines or up to 6 months jail
        - revoked driving licence for 1 year
    
    # Aggravated drunk driving:
        - 1.0 per mille of alcohol in blood; 0.5 mg of alcohol per littler of breath
        - upto 2 years jail
        - revoked driving licence for 2 year. take tests again
    
    # Per mille:
        0.1-0.4% : Loss of certain inhibitions and overestimation of one’s own abilities. Slower reaction time.
        0.4-1.0% : Impairment of vission, speech and coordination
        1.0-2.0% : Uncontrolled body, double vission
        2.0-3.5% : deep sleep
        >3.5% : death
    
    # Awareness:
        - reckless driving but under legal limit is still drunk driving
        - serving alcohol to a driver or lending car to drunk person is punishable offence
        - if taken into custody for being drunk in other context, licence can be revoked
        - legislation of alcohol applies to all motor vehicles
        - impossible to hide alcohol in body
        - you often feel worn out the day after drinking
        - different person can have different level of blood alcohol with same amount of drinking
        - drunk driving rule is included everywhere including fenced off areas and private property
        - alcolock may be an option instead of revoking licence
    
    * If medication makes you a traffic hazar then it's prohibited to drive, you need to judge it. Driving under 
    medication is same as drunk driving

    * Zero tolerance for drugs. Same as drunk driving

    # Mobile phone:
        - handheld mobile phone is always prohibited. Not allowed to hold it.
        - handsfree/gps is prohibited if it makes you traffic hazard
        - texting is also always prohibited and risk level is same as highly drunk person

    # Stats:
        - 20% of traffic deaths are because of alcohol or drugs
        - estimated 15,000 drunk drivers on road every day
        - around 25,000 are caught drunk driving, 15,000 are because of drugs
        - 90% of drunk drivers are men
    
    # Alcohol calculation:
        volume of spirit = % by volume * volume in cl * 0.025
        Example: How many centilitres (cl) of spirits (40% volume) corresponds to 50 cl of low-strength beer (3.5% volume)?
        Answer: 3.5 * 50 * 0.025 = 4.38 ≈ 4 cl


## Tiredness

    * It's forbidden to drive while tired and incapable of driving in safe manner
    * After sleepless night the reaction time is same as a drunk person

    # Cuases:
        - Exhaustion
        - Sleep deprivation
        - Alcohol, drugs, medication
        - Monotony:
            - Wide, long and straight uninterrupted roads
            - Engine/tyre noise
    
    # Dangers:
        - bad concentration/coordination
        - slower reaction time
        - bad decision making
        - difficulty maintaining speed
    
    # Indications:
        - blurred vision
        - dry mouth
        - frequent yawning
        - feeling cold
        - overreacting
        - head feels heavy
        - muscle relaxation
        - optical illusion
        - difficulty to maintain speed
        - problems concentrating
        - difficulty to keep eyes open
    
    # Avoid tiredness:
        - drive when rested
        - not too warm car
        - take break after about an hour
        - do not eat heavy food
    
    # Reduce tiredness:
        * Best way to have long sleep 
        * Temporary:
            - short nap (not less than 15 mins)
            - take break, stretch legs
            - coffee, same as taking a nap
    
    # Stats:
        - most tiredness related accident 2:00 to 5:00
        - most common cause of single vehicle accident
        - 40% accidents outside of buildup area is at night or dawn
        - risk of accident increases with proximity of destination as you feel relax

  
## Vision
    * 180° visual field
    * central vision is 1-2% of visual field, other is peripheral vision

    # Experienced drivers:
        - flexible gaze, smooth transition
        - focus on moving object
        - broader observation
    
    # Inexperienced drivers:
        - Fix gaze on objects and keep it there for long time
        - focus on stationary object
        - narrower observation
    
    * Tunnel vision is when eye scans smaller area and misses what happens on the other side of road
    Tunnel vision increases with: 
        - Tiredness
        - Alcohol 
        - Stress

## Traffic Accidents
    * Olyca == Accident

    # Prone to accidents
        * 15% of the population cause 50% of the accident. Their characteristics:
        - Make excuses from their mistake
        - Deny the danger
        - Act impulsively
        - Are proud
        - Have need for self assertion
        - Are prone to reaction formation
        - Party a lot
        - Adventurous
    
    # If first to arrive at accident:
        - Survey the scene: Number of casualties? Other risks?
        - Priorities: What needs to be done first? Person in a burning car?
        - Warn: If visibility is limited, there is a risk of more cars colliding, thus making the situation worse.
        - Call 112

        # First aid:
            1. Life-threatening situations: if someone is in burning car remove them
            2. Breating: If breating put them recovery position, If not:
                - No pulse: CPR is required
                - Pulse: Mouth to mouth
            3. Bleeding: Apply pressure
            4. Shock: Can have internal bleeding. Make sure person is breatind and do not give them anything to drink
        
        # Remember:
            - Stay at the scheme until emergency responders have taken over. If already people in the scene, then move away
            - Don't move anything unless it's blocking the road
            - Give your information even private person is asking
            - Call police
    
    * Do not approach lorries with dangerous goods

    # Minor accident:
        * Keep an insurance claim form (provided by company). Write down:
        - Time, date & location
        - Name, address of any witness and what they have seen
        - Counterparty's name, address, phone number, vehicle, registration, insurance company
        - Any damage caused
    
    # Parking accident:
        - call the owner. Swedish transport agency can help to find the owner with registration number
        - put a note of your contract information on the windscreen
        - if unable to reach owner then call police.
    
    * DAMAGED SIGN: try to restore it. If you unable to then contract police and warn other road users.

    # Greatest rist of wildlife on road:
        - at dusk and dawn
        - May - June & September - October
        - On salted roads
        - Close to open fields
        - Close to a water caurse
        - at start and end of wildlife fence
    
    * If elk start crossing the road, go behind them

    # What to do if you hit a large animal:
        1. Warn other road users with hazard light and warning triangle
        2. If animal is dead try to move it away from the road.
        3. If animal is wounded you are obliged to call the police if the animal you hit is either:
        Bear, Wolf, Walverine, Lynx, Elk, Deer, Roe deer, Otter, Wild boar, Mouflon sheep, Eagle

    # Warning triangle: 
        - car must be equipped with it
        - if car stalls on a road where the speed is > 50km/h, you must display a warning triangle.
        - place it 50-100 m before

    # Accident locations:
        - most are in built-up areas
        - most serious are outside built-up are

    # Stats:
        - 20% traffic deaths are related to drugs or alcohol
        - 40% killed were not wearing a seat belt
        - 75% killed are men
        - 75,000 accidents occur each year
