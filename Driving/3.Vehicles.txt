
## classification of vehicles
    # Speed for different vehicle:
        - speed limit : Private car, light lorry, motorcycle, light bus
        - 100 : Heavy bus with seat belts
        - 90 : Heavy by without seatbelts, heavy lorry on motorway or clearway
        - 80 : Heavy lorry on the road, vehicle with breaked trailer/caravan, vehicle with unbreaked trailer/caravan
        whose total weight is less than half the pulling vehicle's unladen weight and not more than 750 KG
        - 50 : maximum speed of tractor. Some tractors are designed to be 30km/h
        - 45 : Moped class 1
        - 40 : Vehicle with unbreaked trailer/caravan whose total weight is greater than pulling vehicles service weight
        - 30 : Car towing another car


## Distances
    * Reaction distance is effected by car's speed and your reaction time
    # Calculate the reaction distance
        - Formula: Remove the last digit in the speed, multiply by the reaction time and then by 3.
        - Example of calculation with a speed of 50 km/h and a reaction timeof 1 second:
        5 * 1 * 3 = 15 metres reaction distance
    
    * Breaking distance is effected by vehicle's speed (raised by power of 2), the road, the load, the breaks
    # Calculate the breaking distance
        - Formula: Remove the last digit from the speed, multiply the figure by itself and then multiply by 0.4.
        - Example of calculation with a speed of 10 km/h:
        10 km/h ⇒ 1; 1 * 1 = 1; 1 * 0.4 = 0.4 metres braking distance
    
    # Stopping Distance: Breaking distance + Reaction distance


## Tyres
    # Types:
        - Summer tyres
        - Winter tyres:
            - Studded tyres: 
                - Best on icy surface. 
                - cause a lot of wear on the road
                - older cars without anti-skid system should have studded tyres
            - Friction tyres:
                - as good as studded tyres except on ice.
                - more quite and less wear on the road
                - good for cars with anti-skid system and less winter place
            * bad performance in hot days
        
    # Tread depth:
        - 1-1.6mm : minimum permitted summer tyre
        - 3mm : minimum permitted winter tyre
    
    # When to use winter tyre
        - 1 December to 31 March in winter road condition (legal requirement)
        - Prohibited to have studded tyre 16 April to 30 September (if not winter condition)
    
    # Facts:
        - Correct air pressure is important
        - It is prohibited to combine summer and winter tyres.
        Exception: if winter tyre is punctured, spare summer tyre can be used until winter tyre has been fixed
        Better to change both tyre on same axle for balance.
        - After changing tyre re-tighten the wheel bolts after some tens of kilometers of driving
        - summer tyre + snow chain == winter tyre

    # Tyre fault:
        - Incorrect wheel alignment causes the car to pull on one side
        - Incorrect balancing causes the steering wheel to vibrate
    
    # Spare typre:
        * Regular spare is same tyre. Often best to have an old tyre as spare.
        * A temporary spare is narrower version
    
    # Studded tyre on trailer
        - If car has studded tyre, trailer must have studded tyre
        - If trailer has studded tyre, car does not need to have studded tyre


## Steering:
    # Oversteering: 
        - car turns too much on the back causing rear wheel slide
        - to avoid, put the best tyres at the back
        - normally effects rear wheel drive cars
    
    # Understeering:
        - car turns too little and wants to continue travelling straight
        - normally effects front wheel drive. (new cars are front wheel drive)
        - better than Oversteering
    
    # Electronic stability control
        * Auto corrects the steering with sensors.
        * Makes car stable
        # other names:
            - Anti-skid 
            - Anti-spin
            - DSTC (Dynamic Stability and Traction Control)
            - ESP (Electronic stable program)
            - ESC (Electronic stable control)
    
    # Aquaplaning:
        * Car doesn't have contract with road because of water.
        * Slushplaning is the same thing but with snow or slush

        # Factors that increase:
            - Large pool of water
            - High speed
            - Wide tyres
            - Tyres with poor tread depth
        
        # If it begins:
            - Do not make sudden movement
            - Wheels should point towards roads
            - Depress the clutch
            - Release the accelerator
            - Do not break
    
## Breaks:
    * Dual circuit break system means the breaking system has two different part, if one fails other will work
    * Disk break, Drum break
    * ABS (Anti-lock breaking system) helps you maintain control. Descreases breaking distance. On gravel the 
    breaking distance may increase.

    # Faulty break test:
        Hold down break for 20 secs. if break:
        - sinks very low, means wear
        - continues to sink slowly, means leak in breaking system
        - padel feels springy, means air in breaking system
    
    * Break fluid should be changed every two years.
    * Parking break is to stop the car from rolling. Applies breaks on the back wheels.

## Length & width

        - A load may protrude by maximum 20cm

    # Length:
        - The total maximum length can be 240cm (with or without load)
        - Forward protrusion must be marked. Exception in under 1 meter and clearly visible
        - Rear protrusion must be marked if it protrudes >1m
    
    Marking:

    # Marking trailer:
        # Front:
            - white reflector
            - white position light on trailers wider than 160cm
        # Sides:
            - orange reflectors
            - orange lights
        # Back:
            - rear lights

            - indicator
            - rear registration plate light
            - rear triangle reflector


## Loads
    * It is normally the breaking when load is subjected to pressure

    # correct loading:
        - secure the load. must restrict 80% forward and 50% side movement
        - place it at the front of the boot
        - heaviest part of the load on the bottom
    
    # terminology:
        - Unladen weight: unladen car with driver
        - Maximum load: maximum load permitted in registration
        - total weight: Unladen weight + Maximum load
        - Gross weight: The weight at a given time
    
    * With B licence, you may drive private car or light lorry with a maximum total weight of 3500kg

    # Trailer
        * With B licence, you are allowed to tow a light trailer 

        # A triler is light if:
            - maximum total weight 750 kg 
            OR
            - total weight for car + trailer <= 3500kg

        * Light trailer weight cannot go beyond what car registration say

    # Ball pressure
        * Ball pressure is the pressure trailer puts on the car
        
        - High ball pressure: excesive load on front of the trailer. Poor traction on front wheels of the car.
        - Low ball pressure: excessive load on the back of the trailer. Porr traction with the rear wheels.
    
    # Trailer break:
        - A service break for > 750kg total trailer weight
        - A perking break for > 400kg unladen trailer weight
    
    (see the signs for heavy lorry and heavy trailer)


## Lights
    1. Dipped headlights
        * The norm is to use combination of dipped headlights and parking lights. 
        # Prohibited light combination:
            - Dipped headlights + fog light
            - dipped headlights + daytime running light
            - dipped headlights + auxiliary hight beam light
            - fog lights + daytime running lights
    
    2. Full beam headlights:
        * Most powerful light. Use them as often as you can in the dark.
        # Prohibited if:
            - close incoming any traffic 
            - when behind another vehicle
            - road is not dark
        
        * No need to dim for pedestrians.
        * Good idea to turn off at junction with lot of cars
        * For oncoming lorry, must dim as soon as you see the position lights on the roof
        * Makes bad visible in fog or snow, try switching to the dipped light
        * Fix your gaze on the right hand side of the car

    3. Indicators:
        - turn left or right
        - change lane
        - turn around
        - pull out from the side of the road
        - manoeuvre the car sideways
    
    4. Hazard light:
        - used in time of emergency stop or similar
        - all indicators flashes at the same time
    
    5. Fog light
        - Permitted to use fog light instead of dipped headlight in daylight
    
    6. Rear fog light
        - very powerful red light
        - turn off as soon as the read vehicle has seen you
    
    7. Daytime running light
        - used to increase the car's visibility
        - some cars have it
    
    8. Auxiliary hight beam light
        - Fitted additionally
    
    9. Parking lights
        - should be used when car is stopped or parked in poor visibility
        - low power lights
    
    10. Rear lights

    11. Break lights
        - lights that come when break is pressed

    12. Reversing light
        - lights that come when reverse gear
    
    13. Registration plate light Correct dim 

    # correct dimming of full beam head light
        # Oncoming traffic:
            - keep them on as long as you can
            - when the light meet with incoming cars light or you are dazzled, dim the light
            - exectly when leveled with the car, switch back on 
        
        # Overtaking:
            - keep them on as long as you can
            - when close to the car, dim the light
            - exectly when leveled with the car, switch back full beam
            - Now it's the car that being overtaken must turn off the hight beam, 
        
        # Bends:
            - cars on the lower curve should dim the light
            - cars on the higher curve can keep them on
    
    # Visibility in darkness:
        # dark clothing without reflectors:
            - dipped headlights: 25m
            - high beam: 150m
        
        # light clothing without reflectors:
            - dipped headlights: 60m
            - full beam headlight: 300m
        
        # With reflactor:
            - dipped headlight: 125m
            - full beam: 450m


## Safety checks
    # External Safety checks
        - parking lights, front and rear lights, reflectors, registration plate light
        - turn on ignition, dipped headlight, indicators, rear fog light
        - on left: check front wheel (tread depth, round tyre), front indicator, headlights (fullbeam), rear wheel, 
        rear light, rear indicator and rear for light
        - same on right
        - turn off lights and indicators
        - ask someone to press the break to see break lights
    
    # Internal Safety check
        - correct driving position, check the mirror, check doors are shut
        - check wipers, heating control
        - beep horn once and adjust your seatbelt
        - press and hold the break
        - turn steering wheel slightly and start the car. Power steering should work, break should sink
        - check the handbreak by pulling it and attepting to drive or by releasing it on a slope
    
    # Driving Position
        - Adjust the seat so you should be able to angle your wrists over the sterring wheel
        - Adjust the head restrant so it's positined roughly in the centre of the back of your head
        - Adjust mirrors
        - Fasten seatbelt
        - Depress the clutch
        - Start the engine
        - turn on necessary regulator systems (e.g fans)
        - check correct lights are switched on


## Roadworthiness test
    * Roadworthiness test (inspection) is carried out in order to reduce accident caused by technical fault and to 
    prevent excessive emission of harmful substances

    # Checks
        - Frame: car's structure is not damaged (e.g rusted)
        - Wheels and control system: no damage in front and back wheel. tyre condition and thread depth
        - Drive system: the engine, the electric, exhaust and drive system
        - Break system: functionality, effectiveness and evenness of the break
        - Bodywork: seat belts, windows and doors
        - Communication: lights, indicators, horn, windscreen, washer fluid and warning light
        - environment: exhaust emission
        - Other: towbar, instrument lights, speedometer
    
    * Your responsibility to get the car inspected

    # Inspection time:
        - 1st: 3 years after the month when serviced
        - 2nd: 2 years after the month of 1st inspection
        - subsequent: 14 months after the months it was inspected
    
    # Rules:
        - if not inspected, a driving ban is automatially imposed on the vehile until it's inspectd
        - you must rectify any faults found during inspection otherwise a driving ban will be imposed
        - police can do vehicle spot inspection
        - if vehicle changes (e.g new engine) it must be subjected to registration inspection within 1 month. 


## Services
    # Car battery
        - electric parts (e.g radio/starter motor) run by battery
        - Check the fluid, there is min/max
        - if low, add distilled water. 
        - when engine running batter is charged by genarator
        - battery deteriorates over time
        - fluid in batter is corrosive
    
    # Fuses
        - part of electrical system
        - if electicity has stopped working it's most likely fuse
    
    # Radiator
        - prevents the engine to overheat
        - if engine temp is high, there might be too little coolant.
        - should not open the radiator when engine is warm as it might burn you with hot steam
        - radiator should have coolant which is water and glycol
    
    # Engine Oil
        - lubricant to the engine
        - pull out dipstick, wipe it, dip it again, check the mark
        - added by removing a screw cap on the engine
    
    # Windscreen washer fluid
        - check by looking at the windscreen washer fluid container under the bonnet


## Registration certification
    # PART 1 - information about the vehicle
        # contains:
            - technical information
            - about leasing and purchasing on credit
            - authorisation code for internet service
            - general information about the vehicle
        
        # Used for:
            - notification of on road status
            - notification of off road status
            - Ordering registration plates
            - Ordering Part 2
    
    # PART 2 - certificate of ownership and change of ownership
        # contains:
            - ownership information
            - most recent ownership of the vehicle
            - other general information about the vehicle
        
        # user for:
            - Change of ownership
            - Deregistration
        
        * It's important to keep it somewhere safe. Not in the glove box.
    
    * Chaning of ownership can happen via app or send part 2 via mail

    # Important info:
        - new owner must have third party insurance from the the date specified the change of ownership
        - check one another's personal details. Otherwise Swedish transport agency cannot complete it
    
    # Off-road status
        - by registering as off road, you avoid having to pay vehicle tax or insurance
        - submit by app or part-2 of registration certificate in mail
        - driving ban doesn't mean off road status. need to submit yourself.
        - vehicle with off road status can only be driven to or from roadworthiness test, provided that third-party 
        insurance has been paid and there are no motor vehicle tax arrears.
        - forbidden to tow a vehicle with off-road status


## Insurance
    * Devided into three parts and differs from one insurance to another.
    * keep insurance claim form in the car. order from insurance company

    1. Third-party insurance
        - covers persoan injury and damage to another party's car or property. If own car is damaged, no compensation
        - obligatory
        - if not submitted any claims for number of years, you can receive it cheaper through no-claim bonus
        - if penalised for reckless driving or drunk driving, insurance company may claim money back
    
    2. Partial insurance
        * Covers everything in third-party insurance
        - theft
        - fire
        - glass (e.g stone chips)
        - machine (if engine breaks down)
        - legal protection
    
    3. Fully comprehensive insurance (motor vehicle damage insurance)
        * Covers everything in partial insurance
        - damage to your own car in the event of a traffic accident
        - scratches and dents
        - vandalism
        - salvage and towing
