## Environment
    # Greenhouse Effect:
        - The car makes the carbon dioxide into the atomosphere making it thicker. This thick layer reflect back from the 
        heat rays creating greenhouse effect.

        # Consequences: 
            - Natular disasters
            - Agricultural disturbance
            - Diseases
            - Water shortage

    # Equipment and technology to reduce emission

        # Catalytic converters:
            - transform the harmful substances in exhaust fumes into carbon dioxide and water
            - eliminates 80-95% harmful substances
            - operating temp: 400-600°C

        # Engine heater:
            - catalytic converter starts working
            - lower fuel consumption
            - less wear in the engine

            When to start:
                - +10°C: 30 mins before 
                - 0°C: 1 hour before
                - -10°C: 1.5 hours before
        
        # Reducing air resistence: less fuel consumption
            - remove rails and roof boxes
            - windows shut
            - tyres too wide
        
        # Correct type pressure

        # Using less air conditioning
            - turning off AC can save 5-10% fuel consumption
        
        # Washing car correctly
            - Intended car wash drains water correctly
            - Wax requires less car wash


## Eco driving

    # Avoid first gear
        - appropriate when starting, 
        - gives car a lot of power
        - lot of fuel is used
    
    # Drive in as high a gear as possible
        - most modern car can handle 50km/h in 5th gear
        - if car is slugging go to lower gear
    
    # Accelerate fairly quickly
        - constant speed takes less fuel
        - keep engine below 2500 rpm
    
    # Avoid using clutch
        - The engine uses fuel when the clutch pedal is depressed
    
    # Skip gears

    # Utilize engine braking
        - automatic car handles by itself
        - shift to 1st gear before 1200-1300 rpm
    
    # Avoid breaking and stopping
        - when approaching red light, engine break

## Fuels

    # Petrol and diesel
        - both fossil fuels contribute to greenhouse effect and climate change
        - diesel car has lower fuel consumption
        - diesel car exhaust more dangerous gases
        - at petrol station both are environmental class 1
        - E10 is standard petrol in sweden 
    
    # Electric
        - does not emit exhaust gas
        - battery requires large amount of metal comes from mines
    
    # Hybrid
        - two engines
        - plugin hybrid means it can be charged with cable
    
    # Emission classes
        - Euro classification: Euro 5, Euro 6, etc; higher number means lower emission
        - Other: Electric (“El”), Hybrid (“Hybrid”) and Plug-in hybrid (“Laddhybrid”).
    
    # Environmental zones
        * Class 1 / KLASS 1
            - only effects heavy vehicles
            - newer lorries are allowed but older models are prohibited
            - regular car drives here regardlress of fuel consumption
        
        * Class 2 / KLASS 2
            - Petrol cars: Euro 5 or Euro 6
            - Diesel cars: Euro 6
        
        * Class 3 / KLASS 3
            - electric, gas, fuel cell vehicles allowed
            - petrol, diesel, plugin hybrid prohibited
